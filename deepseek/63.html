❓John decides to get a new apartment with a roommate. His former rent was 2 per square foot for a 750 square foot apartment. If John and his roommate split the rent and utilities cost evenly each month, how much money does John save in a year compared to living in his former apartment? Additionally, the first and last month's rent of the new apartment is prorated based on the number of days John and his roommate occupy the apartment. In the first month, they occupy it for 20 days, and in the last month, they occupy it for 15 days. Calculate John's total savings in a year with all these factors considered.

💡First, calculate the annual cost of the former apartment:- **Monthly Rent**: 750 sq ft * 2/sq ft = 1,500- **Annual Rent**: 1,500 * 12 = 18,000Next, calculate the annual rent for the new apartment:- **First Six Months Rent**: 2,800 * 6 = 16,800- **Last Six Months Rent**: 2,800 * 1.05 = 2,940; 2,940 * 6 = 17,640- **Total Annual Rent**: 16,800 + 17,640 = 34,440Calculate the annual utilities cost:- **Winter Months (3 months)**: 200 * 3 = 600- **Other Months (9 months)**: 150 * 9 = 1,350- **Total Annual Utilities**: 600 + 1,350 = 1,950Adjust for prorated rent:- **First Month (20 days)**: (2,800 / 30) * 20 = 1,866.67- **Last Month (15 days)**: (2,940 / 30) * 15 = 1,470- **Total Prorated Rent**: 1,866.67 + 1,470 = 3,336.67Adjust the total annual rent:- **Adjusted Annual Rent**: 34,440 - (2,800 + 2,940 - 3,336.67) = 29,036.67Calculate the total annual cost for the new apartment:- **Total Cost**: 29,036.67 + 1,950 = 30,986.67Determine John's share:- **John's Share**: 30,986.67 / 2 = 15,493.34Finally, calculate the savings:- **Savings**: 18,000 - 15,493.34 = 2,506.66
